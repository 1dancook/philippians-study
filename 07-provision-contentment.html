    7 - God's provision and Paul's Contentment (4:10-23)

    Main Passage

    Philippians 4:10-23 ESV

    10 I rejoiced in the Lord greatly that now at length you have revived your concern for me. You were indeed concerned for me, but you had no opportunity. 11 Not that I am speaking of being in need, for I have learned in whatever situation I am to be content. 12 I know how to be brought low, and I know how to abound. In any and every circumstance, I have learned the secret of facing plenty and hunger, abundance and need. 13 I can do all things through him who strengthens me.

    14 Yet it was kind of you to share my trouble. 15 And you Philippians yourselves know that in the beginning of the gospel, when I left Macedonia, no church entered into partnership with me in giving and receiving, except you only. 16 Even in Thessalonica you sent me help for my needs once and again. 17 Not that I seek the gift, but I seek the fruit that increases to your credit. 18 I have received full payment, and more. I am well supplied, having received from Epaphroditus the gifts you sent, a fragrant offering, a sacrifice acceptable and pleasing to God. 19 And my God will supply every need of yours according to his riches in glory in Christ Jesus. 20 To our God and Father be glory forever and ever. Amen.

    21 Greet every saint in Christ Jesus. The brothers who are with me greet you. 22 All the saints greet you, especially those of Caesar's household.

    23 The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit.

    Paul expresses his gratitude towards the Philipiian church for their support.

    Extra reading: 2 Corinthians 8 talks about the giving of churches in Macedonia, of which Philippi is a part of.

    Observations and Notes

    • verse 10 - you had no opportunity means that Paul had no need and so they couldn't give.
    • Pauls "abundance" in verse 12 would have been meager and simple. He was an artisan (tent maker) and, although better off than the poor, would have been below the the standard of living enjoyed by modern middle class in the west.
    • fruit in verse 17 is also profit in other translations.
    • letters often included greetings from people other than the author because they were not sent often (they had to be sent via travelers).

    Questions and Application

    1. What did Paul learn about being content? How?

      • in every situation he should be content
      • he learned this through experiencing situations and relying on Jesus

    2. What does `I can do all things through him who strengthens me." mean in its context?

      • Jesus gives me the strength in any situation to be content

    3. Why is it important to be content regarding our needs? Are you content with what you have? Why do we become discontent?

      • If we are not content, we can approach fulfilling our needs in a sinful way
      • we can become discontent by unhealthy desires and jealousy

    4. What observations can you make about the connection of giving and ministry?

      • giving = sharing (4:14)
      • giving = partnership (4:15)
      • the partnership involves giving and receiving (4:15)
      • the giving part involves sending help for needs (4:16)
      • the receiving part is fruit of ministry (4:17) - this could be seen as how investments work. When you invest in something, you get back what you invested with profit.
      • the giving part is an act of worship (4:18)

    5. Read 2 Corinthians 8:1-5. Philippi is among the churches of Macedonia. What do you observe about their giving?

      • they were going through a hard time
      • their giving was not from abundance, but when they were in poverty
      • they were generous
      • they gave beyond their means (v3)
      • it was by God's will
      • these people are being sacrificial and giving beyond what they really should, trusting God to take care of them.

    6. What is the meaning of verse 19 in its context?

      • Paul cannot pay back the Philippians, but God can and he trusts that he will.
      • the promise is broad and yet restricted at the same time - all but only needs
      • it's important to consider who Paul is speaking this to - not in the sense that it only applies to them, but in the sense of their situation. I doubt this promise applies to just anyone, but more specifically it is to those who have been greatly sacrificial in their giving
      • consider also Luke 6:38

    7. What about you? What thoughts do you have about giving your money to those in need (especially those in ministry)?

    8. What is Paul's attitude about receiving gifts?

      • He is not seeking them, but he is seeking that they will receive fruit (in other words, his focus is on the ministry)
